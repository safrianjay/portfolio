01 Context

Why the dashboard mattered, and why the old one wasn't working.

The Challenge

ErudiFi is a fintech platform providing accessible student loans in Southeast Asia. Our users are students and parents navigating complex tuition payment cycles. More than 45% of users dropped off during the application tracking phase. The dashboard was where users were getting lost in their own data.

The Mandate

As Product Designer, I was tasked with creating a centralized dashboard. The goal was to improve transparency in loan status and payment schedules without overwhelming users with financial jargon. The solution needed to be responsive across all devices used by our student demographic.

02 Process

Four stages. Each one built on what the last one found.

User Flow Audit

Stage 01

I mapped out the existing application and payment flows to identify dead ends. I found that users were often confused about their current loan balance and next payment due date, leading to high support ticket volumes.

Cross-Functional Brainstorm

Stage 02

I brought engineering, product, research, business and ops into the room to map out user stories together. Five recurring problems came up independently across different teams, which told us where to focus.

Student Interviews

Stage 03

I spoke with students across our partner universities in the Philippines and Indonesia. I wanted to understand their financial literacy and what information they prioritize when checking their accounts on their phones.

Revamp & Ship

Stage 04

Lo-fi wireframes, then hi-fi prototypes, then moderated usability testing, then handoff. Every design decision traced back to something specific from stages 1 to 3.

03 The Existing Product

Audit of v0

Five key friction points identified during the audit, causing high drop-off rates.

Issue 01

Fragmented information

Users had to navigate multiple screens just to find their next payment due date.

Issue 02

Hidden payment history

Past transactions were buried under several layers of navigation, causing confusion.

Issue 03

Vague application status

"In Progress" wasn't descriptive enough. Users didn't know which document was missing.

Issue 04

Lack of security trust signals

Entering banking details felt risky without clear security badges or encrypted labels.

Issue 05

Complex repayment flow

Too many steps and redirects to external payment gateways without a clear 'success' state.

Key Design Findings

A

Typography

Bigger text, always.

Between quick sessions between classes and varying screen qualities, students requested noticeably clearer hierarchy and readability.

B

Contrast

Strong color contrast.

Price, product name and the primary CTA need to jump off the screen. Subtle grays and soft accents weren't getting read.

C

Copy

Cut the heavy text.

Long descriptions got skipped entirely. Short, scannable bullets, only the parts people actually need to decide.

05 Design Principles

Layout & structure rules

01

Clarity First

The most important data—current balance and next payment—must be visible immediately upon login.

02

Simplified Actions

Common tasks like 'Make a Payment' or 'Upload Document' should be accessible in no more than two taps.

03

Visual Transparency

Use progress bars and status trackers to show exactly where a user is in their loan application lifecycle.

04

Trust & Security

Surface security icons and clear encryption labels during sensitive financial transactions.

06 Wireframes

Lo-fi structural exploration

What changed

  • Unified Header: combined app logo with contextually relevant title and back navigation.
  • Key Stats Up-front: Balance and Due Dates positioned in the most prominent area.
  • Action Cards: used a modular card system for 'Pay Now' and 'Upload Docs' to make them feel distinct.
  • Bottom Navigation: added a sticky bottom nav for quick jumping between Home, Applications, and Profile.

Impact.

"The new dashboard led to a 40% increase in daily active users and a 35% reduction in overdue payments within the first quarter."

Takeaways.

// 01

Prioritize the most needed info.

Users only care about their current status. The dashboard should answer their biggest question in 3 seconds.

// 02

Financial literacy is UX.

Design plays a huge role in demystifying finance. Clear terms and visual progress builds massive user trust.

// 03

Modular systems scale.

By building a card-based dashboard, we made it easy to add new financial products in the future.
