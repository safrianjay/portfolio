01 Context

Why student loan onboarding in the Philippines was fundamentally broken — and why ErudiFi's Bukas needed a rethink.

The Challenge

Bukas is ErudiFi's student tuition financing platform in the Philippines. Most users are 18–24 year olds applying for their first-ever financial product. They're anxious about eligibility, intimidated by the KYC process, and often unsure whether they'll qualify. More than 60% of students who started an application didn't finish it — dropping off at the document upload and credit profiling stages.

The Mandate

As Product Design Lead, I was tasked with rethinking the entire application journey — from the marketing landing page through to loan disbursement. The goal was to reduce drop-off, make the KYC process feel less intimidating, and build enough trust that first-time borrowers could complete the flow confidently on their own.

02 Process

Four research-led stages. Each one fed directly into the next.

Heuristic Evaluation

Stage 01

I walked through the full Bukas application as a student — from homepage to loan disbursement. I flagged every friction point: unclear eligibility messaging, an opaque KYC step, confusing repayment terms, and a "How it works" page that raised more questions than it answered.

User Research & Interviews

Stage 02

I interviewed 12 students from partner universities across Metro Manila. I recruited through campus Facebook groups and screened for students actively looking for tuition financing. Sessions were moderated remotely over Google Meet, with screen sharing.

Usability Testing on UserTesting

Stage 03

I ran moderated sessions on UserTesting with the existing Bukas website. Students talked through their experience live as they navigated the site — this gave us real emotional data: where they hesitated, what confused them, and what made them distrust the platform.

Redesign & Validate

Stage 04

Personas, journey maps, HMW reframes, lo-fi wireframes, hi-fi prototypes, and a second round of usability testing. Every design decision traced back to something a real student said or did in stages 1–3.

03 User Persona

Primary Archetype

Key insights

Insight 01

Parents are the real decision-makers

Nicole won't commit to a loan without parent approval. Bukas needed to speak to both student and parent simultaneously.

Insight 02

Scholarships feel safer than loans

Students don't see themselves as "loan people." Bukas needed to reframe BNPL tuition as a responsible, normal option.

Insight 03

Eligibility anxiety is the #1 blocker

Users worried they wouldn't qualify. They needed reassurance before they'd invest time in the application.

Insight 04

Heavy social media, mobile-first

Nicole uses Instagram, Twitter and Facebook constantly. Discovery happens on social — the app needs to feel familiar from day one.

06 Usability Testing

UserTesting sessions

I ran 8 moderated sessions via UserTesting. Key findings that shaped the redesign:

Finding 01

The "How it works" page confused rather than helped

Students couldn't find answers about eligibility, credit scoring, or what happens if they miss a payment.

Finding 02

ID upload step caused the most abandonment

Students panicked when asked for government ID without context. They feared being rejected or having their data misused.

Finding 03

Repayment terms were buried and unclear

No student could find the answer to "what happens if I can't pay?" — a question every single one had.

Finding 04

"Pay your tuition on time" landed poorly

The hero copy implied the student was already late, rather than offering a proactive solution. Trust dropped before the page fully loaded.

07 Information Architecture

Structural redesign

What changed in the IA

  • Hero reframed: "Here's how to pay your tuition with Bukas" — empowering, not scolding.
  • Requirements upfront so students self-qualify before investing time in the application.
  • Application process broken into clear numbered steps with estimated time per step.
  • Repayments section now answers "what if I miss a payment" directly and prominently.
  • Credit profiling explained in plain language, removing the fear around the scoring process.

08 KYC Flow Redesign

ID verification

The ID verification step was the highest drop-off point. We rebuilt it around trust.

Before

  • ✕ Abrupt ID upload with no context
  • ✕ No explanation of why ID was needed
  • ✕ No progress indicator
  • ✕ Camera permission request felt intrusive

After

  • ✓ Step-by-step progress (1 of 5)
  • ✓ "Why we need this" explainer before each field
  • ✓ Live ID frame with real-time guidance
  • ✓ Clear next step shown after each upload

The
Impact.

"Onboarding completion rose 40%, KYC pass rate jumped 55%, and mid-flow abandonment dropped by nearly a third. The redesign became the foundation for ErudiFi's entire product design system."

By treating first-time borrowers with empathy — explaining every step, reducing anxiety around identity, and making eligibility transparent upfront — we turned a leaky funnel into a confident onboarding experience.

Takeaways.

// 01

Fear is a funnel problem.

Students weren't dropping off because of confusion — they were dropping off because they were scared. Designing for trust matters as much as designing for clarity.

// 02

Explain before you ask.

Every field that asked for sensitive info needed a reason first. "Why we need this" copy added one sentence but reduced abandonment by double digits.

// 03

Parents are users too.

The real decision-maker wasn't the student — it was the parent they'd show the app to that evening. Building for that secondary audience changed everything.
